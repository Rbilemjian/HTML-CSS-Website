In the last year, I have been going to the gym on a consistent basis. It is one of the best habits I have ever picked up. The benefits of hitting the gym consistently are great. More energy, better discipline, meeting all kinds of people and a sense of comradery with gym buddies. It is great and I would higly recommend it to anybody, especially over at the gym I go to which is open 24/7 and only costs $10/month.

The Gym

I go to Crunch Northridge, where membership for just that gym is $10 every month and it is open every day of the week, 24 hours a day. The downside to this gym is that it can get to be pretty crowded. They just recently added a third squat rack, though, so it is a lot less congested especially on leg days.

The Workout

Below I list the exercises within the two types of workout days in the upper/lower body split which I train at the gym.

Upper Body

  1. Stretches and Warm-ups (very important)
  2. Bench Press
  3. Pull-ups
  4. Incline Bench Press (Dumbbell)
  5. Seated Cable Rows
  6. Chest Flies & Lateral Raises (Superset)
  7. Bicep Curl & Tricep Extension (Superset)
  8. Face Pulls
  9. Neck Extension/Contractions

Lower Body

  1. Stretches and Warm-ups (very important)
  2. Front Squat
  3. Deadlifts
  4. Lunges
  5. Calf Raises
  6. Plate Pinches
  7. Hanging Leg Raises
  8. Barbell Grip Endurance Training

The Schedule

This is the schedule that I follow weekly for going to the gym. It used to be upper body 3 times a week, but with school again and how inconsistent that schedule was with days changing all the time, I decided to make it 2 times a week so that the schedule remains constant and I have more time for schoolwork.
